Our Story

We believe that armed with new technology and filled with the urge to feel good while doing good, anyone can make a difference.

Innovation and good intentions are the key! We are unlocking the door and ending inequalities through game-changing, scalable and smart design ideas.

WhiteSave.me founders Dmytri, Jake, and Lulu met while backpacking the Inca trail as part of the “Unnecessary Innovating in the Mountains” gathering in 2013. They soon realized that regardless of the continent, lack of innovative thinking was a problem the world over, as were inequality and lack of privilege for non-White people. Following a weekend of post-it notes, some experimental Ayahuasca, and lots of cold brew coffee, WhiteSave.me was born.

The team’s ah-ha moment came while taking a quick Facebook break and reading 12 facts about White male privilege. Some 40% of Whites (that’s almost 80 million!) in the US believe that non-Whites don’t get ahead because they are not as hard working and motivated as Whites. But it’s been hard up to now for White guys to explain to the rest of the population how to be privileged. Instead, they’ve been made to feel like they should apologize for their privilege, and as if their voices are not as valuable on this subject as those of non-Whites.

We soon found that WhiteSave.me was the answer to our question: How might we resolve inequality? Not all White men are selfish with their privilege. And redistributing privilege equally to everyone in the world has nothing to do with revolution or ‘smashing the White patriarchy’! (LOL!!) Rather, it’s about re-imagining privilege as an infinite resource and enabling White guys to use technology to take the lead in delivering their privilege to the less privileged all over the world! White guys want to share their privilege -- they just haven’t had an app to let them do it! WhiteSave.me is the solution we’ve all been waiting for. A simple app that lets White guys take advantage of their down-time to deliver privilege to the underprivileged right through their devices. Our hundreds of Success Stories from Saviors and Savees all over the world show that it’s working.

The Telekommunist Manifesto is an exploration of class conflict and property, born from a realization of the primacy of economic capacity in social struggles. Emphasis is placed on the distribution of productive assets and their output. The interpretation here is always tethered to an understanding that wealth and power are intrinsically linked, and only through the for- mer can the latter be achieved. As a collective of intellectual workers, the work of Telekom- munisten is very much rooted in the free software and free culture communities. However, a central premise of this Manifesto is that engaging in software development and the produc- tion of immaterial cultural works is not enough. The communization of immaterial property alone cannot change the distribution of material productive assets, and therefore cannot eliminate exploitation; only the self-organization of production by workers can.
